# Motor Policy Terms — What's Covered

## What is Covered (Comprehensive Plan)
- Loss or damage by accident, collision, or impact
- Fire, explosion, self-ignition, or lightning
- Theft, burglary, housebreaking
- Riots, strikes, terrorism, malicious acts
- Earthquake, flood, cyclone, storm, hailstorm, frost, landslide, rockslide
- Damage in transit by road, rail, inland waterway, lift, elevator, or air
- Third-party legal liability (mandatory)
- Personal Accident cover for owner-driver: up to ₹15 lakh

## Add-on Covers (Optional, Extra Premium)

### Zero Depreciation Cover
The insurer does not deduct depreciation on replaced parts during a claim, giving you the full part replacement cost. Recommended for cars under 5 years old.

### Engine Protection Cover
Covers damage to the engine and gearbox from water ingression, oil leakage, hydrostatic lock — events not covered under standard OD.

### Roadside Assistance (RSA)
24x7 help if your car breaks down: towing, fuel delivery, flat tyre service, lockout assistance, minor repairs.

### Return to Invoice (RTI)
If your car is stolen or written off, the insurer pays the original ex-showroom invoice price (instead of just the IDV). Available only for cars up to 3 years old.

### Consumables Cover
Pays for engine oil, gearbox oil, nut, bolt, screws, washers, grease, lubricants, AC gas, distilled water — items normally excluded.

### NCB Protect
Lets you make one claim without losing your NCB at renewal.

## What is NOT Covered (Exclusions)
- Normal wear and tear, mechanical or electrical breakdown, failures
- Damage when driving without a valid driving license
- Damage when driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs
- Damage outside India's geographical area (unless add-on is bought)
- Consequential losses (loss of earnings, hotel stay, etc.)
- Vehicle used contrary to permit (private car used as taxi without commercial cover)
- War, mutiny, nuclear risk
- Depreciation, mechanical wear, and gradual deterioration
- Tyres and tubes — covered only if other parts are also damaged in the same incident, and then at 50% of repair cost
- Battery failure due to age

## Cancellation
You can cancel your policy at any time. Refund is calculated on a short-period (penalty) scale, not pro-rata, unless you've sold the vehicle.

## Policy Transfer (Sale of Vehicle)
When you sell your car, the policy must be transferred to the new owner within 14 days using Form 29/30 from RTO. NCB earned belongs to the seller, not the buyer.

## Renewal
You can renew your policy up to 90 days after expiry without losing accumulated NCB. Beyond 90 days, the policy is treated as new and NCB is lost. After expiry, the insurer may demand a fresh inspection of the vehicle before renewal.
