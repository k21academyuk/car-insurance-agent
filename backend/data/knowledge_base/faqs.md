# Frequently Asked Questions

## Quote & Pricing

### Why is my premium higher than my friend's for the same car?
Your premium depends on multiple factors: vehicle age, IDV, NCB you've accumulated, the RTO/pincode (high-claim zones cost more), engine cc, the plan you choose, and any add-ons. Two identical cars can have very different premiums.

### What's the difference between IDV and ex-showroom price?
Ex-showroom price is what the dealer sells the car for. IDV (Insured Declared Value) is the depreciated current market value used for insurance — always less than ex-showroom price for any car older than 6 months.

### Can I lower my premium?
Yes — pick a higher voluntary deductible, drop unnecessary add-ons, accumulate NCB by being claim-free, install anti-theft devices (some insurers give discounts), and choose Third Party only if your car is very old and you accept the risk.

## Claims

### What should I do immediately after an accident?
1. Make sure everyone is safe; move to the side of the road if possible.
2. Call the police if there's injury, third-party damage, or significant damage. File an FIR.
3. Take clear photos of the damage from multiple angles.
4. Note the other vehicle's number and the driver's details.
5. Inform your insurer within 24 hours via the claims helpline or app.
6. Don't agree to any cash settlement with the other party before talking to your insurer.

### How long does claim settlement take?
Cashless claims at network garages: typically 5–10 days from submitting all documents. Reimbursement claims: 10–20 days. IRDAI mandates settlement within 30 days of receiving complete documentation.

### Will making a small claim affect my NCB?
Yes. Even a single claim wipes out your NCB at renewal. If the claim amount is small (say ₹5,000–10,000) and your NCB discount is significant (35–50%), it's often cheaper to pay out of pocket and retain the NCB. Calculate before deciding.

### What is a deductible?
The amount you pay out of pocket for every claim before insurance kicks in. There are two types:
- **Compulsory deductible**: Set by IRDAI, usually ₹1,000–₹2,500 depending on engine cc.
- **Voluntary deductible**: You choose to pay more upfront in exchange for a lower premium.

## Policy & Process

### Can I transfer my NCB to a new car?
Yes. NCB is tied to you, not the car. When buying a new car, ask your existing insurer for an NCB Retention Letter — it's valid for 3 years and gives you the same NCB discount on the new policy.

### My policy expired 2 months ago. Can I renew?
Yes, you can renew up to 90 days after expiry without losing your accumulated NCB. Beyond 90 days, the policy is treated as new and you lose all NCB. The insurer may also demand a fresh vehicle inspection.

### Can I drive the car the day after my policy expires?
No. Driving without valid third-party insurance is illegal under the Motor Vehicles Act and attracts a fine up to ₹2,000 and/or imprisonment for first-time offenders.

### What is Zero Depreciation cover?
An add-on where the insurer doesn't deduct depreciation on replaced parts during a claim. With standard comprehensive cover, you absorb 30–50% of the part cost as depreciation. With Zero-Dep, you get full replacement cost (minus deductible). Best for cars less than 5 years old.

### Are riots and natural disasters covered?
Comprehensive cover includes riots, strikes, malicious acts, floods, earthquakes, cyclones, and hailstorms. Third-party only does not cover any of these — only your liability to others.
