# IRDAI Motor Insurance Guidelines (Summary)

## Mandatory Coverage
Third-party liability cover is mandatory under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. Driving without a valid third-party motor insurance policy is a punishable offence with a fine up to ₹2,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 months for first offence, and up to ₹4,000 for repeat offences.

Own Damage cover is optional but strongly recommended. Without OD cover, your insurer will not pay for damage to your own vehicle from accident, fire, theft, or natural calamities.

## IDV (Insured Declared Value)
IDV is the maximum amount your insurer will pay if your car is stolen or completely written off. It's calculated as the manufacturer's listed selling price minus depreciation per IRDAI slab:

- 0–6 months: 5% depreciation
- 6 months–1 year: 15%
- 1–2 years: 20%
- 2–3 years: 30%
- 3–4 years: 40%
- 4–5 years: 50%
- 5+ years: mutually agreed between insurer and insured

A higher IDV means a higher premium but better protection in case of total loss.

## NCB (No Claim Bonus)
NCB is a reward for not making a claim during a policy year. It's a discount on the Own Damage premium at renewal:

- After 1 claim-free year: 20%
- After 2 claim-free years: 25%
- After 3 claim-free years: 35%
- After 4 claim-free years: 45%
- After 5+ claim-free years: 50%

NCB is tied to the policyholder, not the vehicle. You can transfer it to a new car via an NCB retention letter (valid for up to 3 years).

A single claim resets your NCB to 0.

## Claim Settlement Timeline
- Insurer must acknowledge the claim within 7 days of intimation
- A surveyor is appointed within 24–72 hours for claims above ₹50,000
- Final settlement within 30 days of submission of all required documents

## Required Documents for a Claim
1. Duly filled and signed claim form
2. Copy of policy document
3. Driving license of the driver at the time of incident
4. RC (Registration Certificate) copy
5. FIR copy (mandatory for theft, third-party damage, and major accidents)
6. Repair estimate from the garage
7. Damage photos
8. Original repair bills and payment receipts (for reimbursement claims)

## Cashless vs Reimbursement Claims
- **Cashless**: At a network garage. Insurer settles the bill directly. Customer pays only deductible + depreciation amount.
- **Reimbursement**: At any garage. Customer pays upfront and submits bills for reimbursement (takes 7–15 days).

## Geographical Coverage
Standard policies cover incidents within India only. For Bhutan, Nepal, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Pakistan, or Maldives, you need a special add-on.
